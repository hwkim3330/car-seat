Section 01Introduction: The Paradigm Shift in Vehicle Interiors

The automotive industry stands at the nexus of a profound transformation, commonly framed through the CASE paradigm—Connected, Autonomous, Shared, and Electrified. Within this macro-shift, the vehicle interior, and particularly the seat, is no longer a passive safety fixture or a simple seating apparatus. The modern automotive seat is evolving into a sophisticated mechatronic system capable of sensing occupant biosignals for health monitoring, providing active pneumatic feedback for fatigue management, and integrating with smartphone applications for remote personalization.

This report delivers an in-depth analysis of the smart seat monitoring and control ecosystem, encompassing OEM-centric application platforms (Genesis Connected Services, Kia Connect), key industry players (Hyundai Transys, LG Electronics, KATECH, ETRI), underlying sensing and actuation technologies, and government-led R&D funding directions. The objective is to provide a comprehensive knowledge base for technology developers, policy planners, and research organizations operating in this rapidly converging domain.

Key Insight

The automotive seat is transitioning from a mechanical component to a healthcare platform—a sensor-rich, cloud-connected interface that will serve as the primary touchpoint between occupants and an ecosystem of health, comfort, and safety services.

2.1 Non-Intrusive Biosignal Monitoring

The hallmark of smart seat monitoring lies in "unconstrained, unconscious" sensing—acquiring physiological data from occupants without requiring wearable devices, solely through the act of sitting.

Electrocardiogram (ECG) & Ballistocardiogram (BCG)

Based on research trends from KATECH (Korea Automotive Technology Institute) and ETRI (Electronics and Telecommunications Research Institute), the technical foundation rests on the fusion of capacitive and piezoelectric sensor modalities:

  • Capacitive ECG: Electrodes embedded in the seatback detect cardiac electrical activity through clothing. This enables extraction of heart rate (HR) and heart rate variability (HRV) for stress indexing and drowsiness classification.
  • Ballistocardiogram (BCG): High-sensitivity piezoelectric film sensors or load cells detect micro-mechanical recoil forces generated by cardiac ejection. BCG provides a crucial complementary channel in electromagnetically noisy vehicle environments.
  • Respiratory Rate: Thoracic expansion/contraction patterns are derived from seat pressure fluctuations, serving as one of the earliest predictive indicators for sleep-onset transitions.

Pressure Mapping & Posture Analysis

Companies such as Algorigo (a specialized startup) and Hyundai Transys (Tier 1 supplier) leverage distributed pressure sensor matrices across seat cushions and backrests:

Asymmetric Posture Detection

Real-time identification of lateral leaning or spinal misalignment—primary contributors to musculoskeletal fatigue during extended driving. Triggers corrective notifications or automatic air bladder adjustment.

Occupant Classification (OCS)

Distinguishes between adults, children, and child restraint systems (CRS). Supports FMVSS 208 compliance for airbag deployment modulation and enables "rear-seat occupant alert" app features.

2.2 Active Actuation Systems

Monitored data is translated into physical seat reconfiguration through active feedback mechanisms. The current state-of-the-art is exemplified by Hyundai Transys's Ergo Motion Seat technology.

Pneumatic Control Architecture

Unlike conventional motor-driven systems, which suffer from slow response times and limited pressure resolution, the Ergo Motion Seat employs seven independently controlled air bladders:

  • Micro-Stretching: Cushion bladders expand up to 10 mm, lumbar bladders up to 30 mm, delivering continuous massage-like stimulation to improve blood circulation. Clinical validation was performed in collaboration with KRISS (Korea Research Institute of Standards and Science).
  • Driving Mode Integration: Through CAN bus communication, the seat receives real-time driving mode state. In Sport mode, side bolster bladders inflate within 2 seconds for enhanced lateral support—a hybrid control approach combining app-defined presets with live vehicle dynamics.
Clinical Evidence

Collaborative studies between Hyundai Transys and KRISS demonstrated that pneumatic micro-stretching cycles reduced lower-back muscle fatigue by 23% and improved subjective comfort scores by 31% during 2-hour simulated driving sessions.

Section 03Mobile App-Based Seat Control & Connected Services

In the current market landscape, smart seat control is predominantly delivered through OEM-integrated connected service platforms rather than standalone third-party applications. This architectural choice reflects stringent vehicle security requirements and the necessity for deep system-level integration.

3.1 Genesis Connected Services

Deployed across flagship models (G90, GV80), Genesis Connected Services establishes the benchmark for remote seat and climate integration.

Remote Climate & Seat Integration

The Genesis Intelligent Assistant App enables comprehensive pre-boarding environment customization through a unified remote start workflow:

  • Scenario-Based Presets: Users define seasonal profiles (e.g., "Winter Commute") that bundle target cabin temperature (22°C), seat heater activation (Level 2), and steering wheel heater state into a single command.
  • Granular Control: Beyond binary on/off states, newer models expose three-level intensity control for both heated and ventilated seats within the app. Commands propagate from the Telematics Modem Unit (TMU) to the Body Control Module (BCM) and finally to the Seat ECU.

Personalization & Profile Roaming

  • Smart Posture Care: Occupants input height, weight, and seated height; an optimization algorithm computes the ergonomically optimal seat position and executes automatic adjustment.
  • Cloud Profile Roaming: Seat configuration data is persisted to OEM cloud infrastructure, enabling seamless profile restoration across different Genesis vehicles upon user authentication.

3.2 Kia Connect & Relaxion Comfort

Relaxion Comfort Seat Control

Featured prominently in the EV6 and EV9, this system delivers a zero-gravity recline mode optimized for charging-time rest periods. The Kia Access App enables simultaneous monitoring of charge state alongside interior climate and seat configuration. While in-motion seat adjustments remain restricted to physical controls for safety reasons, app control authority is expanding for stationary/charging scenarios.

Security Integration

Though not a direct seat function, occupancy sensors contribute to the broader security fabric: Valet Mode activation monitors for anomalous seat operations, triggering push notifications to the owner's device when unexpected state changes are detected.

Section 06Conclusions & Strategic Implications

This analysis reveals three macro-trends that will define the trajectory of smart seat technology over the coming decade:

1. App Integration

Seat control is being deeply embedded within OEM super-apps (Genesis Connected, Kia Connect) rather than distributed as standalone applications. This reflects security imperatives and the necessity for cross-system orchestration with HVAC, lighting, and powertrain.

2. Healthcare Evolution

Monitoring is advancing from posture correction to clinical-grade biosignal acquisition. KATECH and ETRI research outputs, once productionized through Hyundai Transys, will enable "Driver Health Reports" delivered directly to mobile devices.

3. Data-Driven Services

Aligned with government data ecosystem initiatives, seat-sourced lifelogs will catalyze new business models in usage-based insurance (UBI), preventive healthcare, and personalized content recommendation.

4. Service-Centric R&D

Organizations pursuing related R&D must adopt service scenario–centric approaches—designing for the Vehicle–Cloud–Mobile triad rather than isolated component optimization.

Strategic Recommendation

For R&D planners and technology developers: the competitive differentiator is no longer the individual sensor or actuator, but the end-to-end service architecture that organically connects Vehicle ↔ Cloud ↔ Mobile. Future grant proposals and product roadmaps should center on integrated user-experience scenarios rather than isolated component specifications.
